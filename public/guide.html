What is CR Analytics?

CR Analytics is a scouting and performance dashboard built for the 2026 PCH District season. It pulls live data from three external sources — The Blue Alliance (TBA), Statbotics, and Peekorobo — and presents them in one unified view optimized for competition day use.

Data is stored in a data.json file and refreshed on demand by the site administrator. You do not need to do anything to keep the data current — simply reload the page to pick up the latest refresh.

Live event days: The app prioritizes the currently active event across all tabs automatically. Live webcasts appear on the Overview tab when an event is in progress.

Data Sources

Filtering

The status bar contains two groups of filter pills on the right side, separated by a thin divider:

Match type pills (green) — Quals · Playoffs · All

Controls which match type is shown across the entire site. The active pill is filled green. This affects: Scoring Analysis, Team Tracker cards, Match Log, and the Overview phase stats. Defaults to All on each page load.

Event pills (blue) — DAL · GWI · COL · ALB · GAI · CMP

Filters all statistics and views to one or more specific events. Click a pill to activate it — click again to deselect. Multiple pills can be active simultaneously. Active pills are filled blue. Unloaded events appear dimmed and are not clickable.

The event filter applies to: Overview, District Rankings, Scoring Analysis, Team Tracker, Alliances & Playoffs, and Match Log.

Event Standings is not affected by either pill filter. It has its own independent event selector dropdown.

Sortable columns

Every data table across the site supports click-to-sort on column headers — District Rankings, Scoring Analysis, Team Tracker (All Teams), Alliances & Playoffs, Match Log, and Event Standings. Click any column header once to sort ascending, click again to sort descending. There is no visual indicator on the header itself; the sort is applied immediately to the rows. Each table returns to its default sort order on page reload.

Team Tracker

Adding & removing teams

  • Enter a team number in the input box at the top of the Team Tracker tab and press Enter or click + Add.
  • Alternatively, click + Track next to any team in the All Teams table at the bottom of the tab.
  • Up to 6 teams can be tracked simultaneously.
  • To remove a team, click the ✕ Remove button in the top-right corner of the team's card.

Color coding

Each tracked team is automatically assigned one of six colors: Green Blue Amber Purple Coral Pink. The color follows the card's position — drag cards to reorder them and the colors reassign accordingly. Tracked team colors appear as highlights throughout all tabs.

Card controls

  • Avg: All / Last 10 / Trimmed — controls which matches are included in the phase averages. Trimmed removes the 2 highest and 2 lowest scores. This setting is per-card and does not affect other cards.
  • Match type (Quals / Playoffs / All) is controlled globally by the status bar pills and applies to all cards simultaneously — see the Filtering section.

Analytics toggles

Each card has four independent toggle buttons in the top-right corner — OPR, EPA, ACE, and Shifts. Each button is highlighted green when active. Toggling any one of them turns that section on or off across all tracked cards simultaneously.

Alliance-level note: OPR and the Shifts breakdown reflect alliance-level scoring during matches when that team was present — not individual robot contributions. Use EPA and ACE for individual contribution estimates.

Match Log

The Match Log shows all played and scheduled matches across every PCH event. The event with the most upcoming unplayed matches sorts to the top automatically — during competition this means the active event is always first.

Match time estimates

Unplayed matches show TBA's estimated start time (e.g. 9:30 AM) in the video column instead of a YouTube link. Once a match is played and a video is available, the link replaces the time.

ACE win predictions

When Peekorobo ACE data is loaded, each match row shows two columns on the right:

  • Pred — the predicted winning alliance with probability % based on the sum of each alliance's ACE scores.
  • Result — the actual winning alliance badge (RED / BLUE / TIE).
  • A ⚡ bolt icon appears when the underdog won.

Phase breakdown (click to expand)

Click any played match row to expand a full phase breakdown panel. The panel shows Red Alliance on the left and Blue Alliance on the right, with each phase as a labeled row in the center:

  • Auto · Transition · Shift 1/2 · Shift 3/4 · Endgame
  • Teleop (subtotal of Transition + Shifts + Endgame)
  • Total

Each row has proportional filled bars so you can visually compare phase performance at a glance. Shift 1 and Shift 2 are combined into one row (as are Shift 3 and 4) since each alliance scores in one or the other, never both.

Click the row again to collapse the panel.

Filtering

Use the Filter by team # input above the table to narrow matches to a specific team. Match type (Quals / Playoffs / All) and event filtering are both controlled by the global status bar pills.

Match Track

Every match row has a Match Track button on the right. Clicking it instantly clears any currently tracked teams and loads all six teams from that match into the Team Tracker — red alliance teams first (slots 1–3), then blue alliance teams (slots 4–6). This lets you quickly pivot the Team Tracker, Scoring Analysis, and Match History views to focus on a specific match's participants without manually adding each team.

  • Tracked team cards display a Red Alliance or Blue Alliance badge in the appropriate color while Match Track is active.
  • The active Match Track row is highlighted with a green ★ Tracking button. Clicking it again clears tracking.
  • Manually removing any tracked team clears Match Track mode and all alliance badges.
  • Clicking Match Track on a different row replaces all six teams with the new match's participants.
  • Tracked teams are cleared automatically when switching between PCH and Championship modes.

Event Standings

The Event Standings tab provides a detailed per-event rankings view that combines TBA's official data with Statbotics EPA and Peekorobo ACE.

Column groups

  • Blue columns (Event data) — Ranking Score (RS), game-specific sort order metrics (e.g. Avg Match, Avg Auto Fuel, Avg Tower), Total Ranking Points, Avg Alliance Score, and Avg Auto. All sourced from TBA for the selected event.
  • Green columns (Season data) — EPA Total (Statbotics), ACE (Peekorobo), and ACE Confidence. These are season-level metrics and reflect the team's performance across all events.

Event selector

The dropdown at the top of the tab lets you switch between events. This selector is independent of the site-wide event pill filter — changing the pills won't change the standings view, and vice versa. The tab defaults to the live event when one is active, or to the most recently completed event otherwise.

EPA vs ACE vs OPR

OPR — Offensive Power Rating (The Blue Alliance)

OPR is a linear regression model that estimates how many points each team contributes to their alliance's score per match at a specific event. It is calculated from match scores alone without any prior knowledge of individual robot actions. Two companion metrics are also available:

  • DPR (Defensive Power Rating) — estimates how many points the team prevented the opposing alliance from scoring.
  • CCWM (Calculated Contribution to Winning Margin) — OPR minus DPR, representing the team's net impact on the score margin.

OPR is event-specific and resets at each event. It is most reliable after 6+ qualification matches have been played.

EPA — Expected Points Added (Statbotics)

EPA is computed directly from score breakdowns and measures how many points above average a team contributes per match. It is broken down into Auto, Teleop, and Endgame components. Event-specific EPA reflects performance at a single event, while the season EPA aggregates across all events. EPA updates in near-real-time and is highly accurate for measuring raw scoring contribution.

ACE — Adjusted Contribution Estimate (Peekorobo)

ACE multiplies a team's raw contribution estimate by a confidence score (shown as a percentage) that adjusts for:

  • Consistency — teams that perform reliably across all matches score higher
  • Win rate alignment — how well the team's contribution predicts wins
  • Veteran status & event count — more events played = higher confidence

ACE tends to reward consistent, reliable veterans and penalize rookie or inconsistent teams relative to their raw scores. State, US, district, and global rankings in the app are all based on ACE.

Which to use? Use OPR for a quick, event-specific scoring estimate based purely on match results. Use EPA for the most precise measure of individual phase contributions (Auto/Teleop/Endgame) across the season. Use ACE to assess overall competitive reliability and predict consistent alliance performance — it rewards veterans and penalizes inconsistency.

Houston Championship Mode

The FIRST Championship Division view is a dedicated scouting experience for the 2026 FIRST Championship (April 29 – May 1, Houston, TX). It covers all teams competing in Team 1002's assigned division, drawing on pre-championship event results, Statbotics, and Peekorobo.

Switching modes

Use the Houston Mode toggle button in the status bar to switch between Peachtree District view and FIRST Championship Division view. The button is visible once Houston Mode is enabled. Refreshing the page while in Championship mode will keep you in that view.

Data is independent: The Championship view has its own dataset separate from the PCH district data. Switching modes does not affect either dataset.

Pre-event vs. live-event states

Houston Mode has two display states that switch automatically based on whether division match data is available:

  • Pre-event — shows pre-championship season data for all division teams (EPA, ACE, OPR, rankings, match history from their last 2026 events). The Overview tab shows a division assignment banner and Team 1002's full PCH season record.
  • Live-event — once division qualification matches begin, all tabs switch to division match data. Livestream embeds appear on the Overview tab, and division standings, match log, and scoring analysis all update in real time.

Match type filter

The Quals / Playoffs / All pills in the status bar work the same as in PCH mode — they filter all tabs simultaneously by match type.

Houston Team Tracking & Match Track

The Team Tracking popup

In Houston Mode, team selection is handled through a dedicated popup rather than a text input. Click the Team Tracking button in the top-right corner of the status bar to open it. The popup lists all teams in the division with their EPA and global rank. Click any team to add or remove them from your tracked list.

Capacity and colors

Up to 9 teams can be tracked simultaneously in Houston Mode (vs 6 in PCH mode). Each slot is assigned one of nine colors: Green Blue Amber Purple Coral Pink Cyan Lime Gold. When a team is added it fills the next available slot. The popup shows a count badge and color dots for all currently tracked teams. Once 9 teams are tracked, unselected teams in the popup are disabled until one is removed.

Color persistence across tabs

Tracked team numbers appear in their assigned color throughout all Houston Mode tabs — in the Global Rankings table, within both alliance columns in Scoring Analysis, and in Match Log rows. This makes it easy to spot your teams at a glance regardless of which tab you are on.

Reordering

Drag tracked team cards on the Team Tracker tab to reorder them. Colors follow position — reordering reassigns colors to keep the most prominent teams in the most visible color slots.

Match Track

The [Division] Matches tab has a Match Track button on every row. Clicking it clears all current tracked teams and loads the six teams from that match — red alliance teams first (slots 1–3), then blue alliance teams (slots 4–6). This is the fastest way to load a specific upcoming or recent match for analysis.

  • Team cards show a Red Alliance or Blue Alliance badge (in red or blue) while Match Track is active.
  • The badge also appears in the Team Tracker summary table next to the team name.
  • Clicking the active match's ★ Tracking button clears all six teams.
  • Clicking Match Track on a different match replaces all six with the new match's teams.
  • Manually removing any tracked team exits Match Track mode and clears all badges.

Tracked teams and mode switching

Tracked teams do not persist between modes. Switching from PCH to Championship mode (or back) automatically clears all tracked teams, alliance badges, and any active Match Track state — giving you a clean slate in the new mode.

Livestream management

On the Overview tab during the live event, two stream embeds appear — Overhead View and Field View. Click the pencil ✎ icon next to either stream to enter a YouTube URL or video ID. The stream saves to your browser session and persists across tab switches. To update a stream URL mid-event, click the pencil icon again.

Streams are session-local: Livestream URLs are stored in your browser's session storage and are not shared with other users or persisted across page reloads. You will need to re-enter stream URLs if you close and reopen the browser.
